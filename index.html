— The Great History of Medieval Warriors —

The Middle Ages were an era when steel decided the fate of nations, and the sword was the ultimate symbol of honour and power. From the 5th to the 15th century, great warrior cultures flourished across the entire planet: European knights with their chivalric code, Japanese samurai walking the path of Bushido, Arab warriors of the faith, Mongol horsemen who conquered half the world, Vikings who terrorised entire continents, and the legendary Kyrgyz hero Manas, who united forty tribes into an indestructible people. Each of these traditions created an image of the warrior that lives through the ages — not only in the pages of historical chronicles, but in legends, art, and the hearts of people to this day.

The Great Warriors

— Legends That Changed History —

Manas Batyr
🏔 Kyrgyzstan · IX–X c.

The great epic hero of the Kyrgyz people. United forty tribes, liberated his homeland from enemies. His name became a symbol of unity, courage and freedom.

🏔 Kyrgyz Batyr
Saladin (Salah ad-Din)
🌙 Egypt · 1137–1193

Sultan of Egypt and Syria who liberated Jerusalem from the Crusaders in 1187. Renowned not only for military genius but for his extraordinary nobility towards defeated enemies.

🌙 Kurdish Sultan
Sultan Baibars
🌙 Egypt · 1223–1277

A Mamluk warrior from the Kipchak steppes. Defeated the Mongols at Ain Jalut — the first defeat of the Mongols in history. Destroyed the Crusader states and built a mighty empire.

🌙 Mamluk Sultan
Ragnar Lothbrok
⚡ Scandinavia · IX century

The legendary Viking king who spread terror across France and England. His sons created the Great Heathen Army that conquered half of the British Isles.

⚡ Norse Jarl
Miyamoto Musashi
⛩ Japan · 1584–1645

The greatest samurai of Japan. Fought 61 duels and never lost a single one. Author of The Book of Five Rings — a masterpiece of martial strategy studied to this day.

⛩ Japanese Samurai
Genghis Khan
🏹 Mongolia · 1162–1227

The greatest conqueror in human history. Created an empire spanning 24 million km² — from the Pacific Ocean to Eastern Europe.

🏹 Mongol Khagan
Khalid ibn al-Walid
☪ Arabia · 585–642

The undefeated commander of Islam. Fought over 100 battles without a single defeat. Named "The Sword of Allah" by the Prophet Muhammad himself.

☪ Sword of Allah
Richard I the Lionheart
✝ England · 1157–1199

The Crusader-King, a legend of the chivalric age. His military campaigns in the Holy Land and his rivalry with Saladin entered the textbooks of military history.

✝ Crusader King

Fascinating Facts

— Secrets and Mysteries of Medieval Warriors —

⚔

Weight of a Knight's Armour

A full suit of plate armour from the 14th–15th centuries weighed between 15 and 25 kg. Despite this, knights could run, jump, and mount their horse entirely unassisted.

🗡

The Samurai's Katana

Forging a katana took up to 18 months. The swordsmith would fold the steel more than 30,000 times. The blade was so sharp it could slice through silk falling onto it.

🏹

The Mongol Bow

The composite Mongol bow had a range exceeding 500 metres with accuracy up to 300 m. A Mongol horseman could loose 10–12 arrows per minute at full gallop.

🏔

The Epic of Manas

The Epic of Manas is the longest epic poem in the world — twenty times longer than the Iliad and the Odyssey combined. Manaschi bards could recite it for days without stopping.

⚡

Viking Berserkers

Elite berserker warriors entered a battle trance and felt no pain. Scholars believe this was induced by fly agaric mushrooms or a deep psychological trigger trained since childhood.

🌙

Saladin's Nobility

After capturing Jerusalem, Saladin freed thousands of Crusaders. He sent fresh horses and a personal physician to his enemy Richard I when the king fell gravely ill during the campaign.

🔱

Bushido — The Way of the Warrior

The samurai code of Bushido placed death above life in matters of honour. A warrior who lost his honour performed seppuku. This path forged a singular mindset — fearlessness before death.

⛪

The Templars — Bankers of War

The Knights Templar invented the first banking system of the Middle Ages. Crusaders could deposit money in France and withdraw it in Jerusalem without transporting any gold.

Chronicle of the Age

— Key Events of the Medieval Wars —

732 AD
Battle of Tours
Charles Martel halted the Arab conquest of Europe. This battle determined the fate of Western civilisation.
IX–X century
The Age of Manas
The legendary hero Manas unites forty Kyrgyz tribes. The birth of a great epic that became the soul of an entire people.
845 AD
Ragnar Seizes Paris
Vikings sail up the Seine and besiege Paris. The Frankish king pays an enormous ransom — 7,000 pounds of silver.
1187 AD
Battle of Hattin
Saladin destroys the Crusader army. Jerusalem falls. The news shook all of Europe and triggered the Third Crusade.
1206 AD
Birth of the Mongol Empire
Temujin is proclaimed Genghis Khan. The beginning of the greatest conquests in history — from the Pacific to the Atlantic.
1260 AD
Ain Jalut — End of Mongol Expansion
Baibars inflicts the first major defeat on the Mongols. The tide of conquest is stopped forever.
1612 AD
Musashi's Final Duel
Miyamoto Musashi defeats Sasaki Kojiro on the island of Ganryu. His 61st duel — once more, victory. The legend is complete.
